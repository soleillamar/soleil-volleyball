Volleyball Positions

About Volleyball Positions Fun Facts

Positions

Setters:

Setters often receive the second touch and sets it to an attacking player. They are like the leader of "offense". Setters also need to be prepared to receive opposing team's attacks near the net.

Spikers (Hitters):

Spikers are the attacking player in volleyball. After receiving a set from another player, they preform attacks directly to the opposing team. Front-row spikers can also block the opposing team's attacks.

Overview

Volleyball consists of multiple positions, all are necessary for the game.

Indoor Volleyball

  • Contains the positions listed
  • When there is one setter on the court it is called 5:1
  • When there are two setters on the court (6:2) one is located in the back row while the other is in the front

Beach Volleyball

  • Only two people at a time, so all positions are played by both players
  • One receives, other sets, other attacks

Positions

Receivers:

Receivers are all about defense. They are prepared to hit any ball that may come there way. Unlike liberos, back-row receivers can spike a ball over the net. Usually, recievers pass the ball to the setter, setting up an attack.

Liberos:

Liberos fall under the receiver class. They can not spike if the ball is anywhere over the net. Liberos do not serve at any time in the game. Liberos are often the most equipped skilled defensive player in the game
